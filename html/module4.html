Transactions

Blockchain transactions are secure, peer-to-peer transfers of digital assets recorded on a decentralized, immutable ledger. Initiated by users via digital wallets, these transactions are broadcast, verified by network nodes for validity, grouped into cryptographically secured blocks, and finalized through consensus mechanisms (like Proof of Work or Proof of Stake).

Key Aspects of Blockchain Transactions

Process:Initiated -> Broadcast -> Validated -> Added to a block -> Confirmed/Finalized.
Security:Transactions are signed with digital signatures, ensuring only the owner can initiate them.
Immutability: Once added to a block and linked to the chain, records cannot be altered, making them highly secure.
Transparency: All nodes (computers) in the network typically have access to the ledger, allowing for transparent, auditable records.
Decentralization:Transactions occur directly between users, removing the need for a trusted third-party intermediary, such as a bank.
Finality: Once a block is added, the transaction is considered confirmed and generally irreversible.

Components of a Transaction

Sender and Recipient: The addresses of the wallets involved.
Transaction Value: The amount being transferred.
Digital Signature: A unique identifier confirming authorization.
Mempool: The waiting area where unconfirmed transactions wait to be added to a block.
