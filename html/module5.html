Mining

Blockchain mining is the decentralized process of validating transactions and securing proof-of-work networks (like Bitcoin) by solving complex mathematical puzzles. Miners use powerful computers to verify transactions, adding them as new blocks to the blockchain, and are rewarded with newly created cryptocurrency and transaction fees.

Key Aspects of Blockchain Mining

Purpose: Mining secures the network against fraud and ensures decentralized, trustless operation.
Rewards: Miners receive newly minted cryptocurrency and transaction fees as compensation for their energy and hardware costs.
Proof of Work (PoW): Miners (nodes) compete to solve cryptographic puzzles using specialized hardware (e.g., ASICs). The first to solve the puzzle adds the block, according to Investopedia.
Mining Pools: To combat high competition, miners often join forces in pools, combining computing power and sharing rewards.
Risks: Mining is energy-intensive, susceptible to high electricity costs, and poses risks of centralization if too few entities control the majority of hashing power.

How the Process Works

Transaction Verification: Transactions are submitted, and miners group them into a new "candidate block".
Solving the Puzzle: Miners search for a specific hash (a "nonce") to secure the block, as shown on this PDF.
Validation & Addition: The first miner to solve the puzzle broadcasts the result. Other nodes verify it, and the block is officially added to the chain.
Reward Distribution: The miner gets a block reward (e.g., 6.25 Bitcoins currently) and transaction fees, notes this YouTube video.
